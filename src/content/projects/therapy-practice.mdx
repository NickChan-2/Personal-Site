## The situation

Starting therapy is personal. Before a prospective client ever reaches out, the website needs to answer practical questions while helping the experience feel safe, calm, and understandable.

This project is for a new private practice that needs a credible digital foundation—not a dense brochure or an aggressive sales funnel. The site should introduce the practice, explain how support works, and offer a low-friction path to a general consultation request.

<InsightPanel label="Core design question">
  How can the website reduce uncertainty without pretending a sensitive,
  personal decision is simple?
</InsightPanel>

## The approach

The structure begins with the questions a prospective client is likely to carry: Who is this for? What kind of support is offered? What can I expect? How do I begin?

The visual direction uses generous space, restrained color, and a softer editorial rhythm. Important actions remain easy to find, but they do not overpower the content. Trust comes from clarity, tone, and consistency rather than unsupported promises.

### A carefully framed first step

The inquiry experience will collect only the information needed to start a general conversation. It will avoid requesting clinical details and will make the limits of a website form clear before submission.

### Built for continued care

The practice should not need to become its own web team. After launch, the technical layer, hosting, and routine updates will remain managed so the site can change as the practice grows.

## Current state

This is active client work and is intentionally labeled **In development**. Strategy, page structure, and the trust-first design direction are established. Final content, approved imagery, privacy language, and integrations will be completed with the client before launch.

No performance outcomes are being claimed while the website is still in progress. This case study will be updated when the work is live and there is real evidence to share.
